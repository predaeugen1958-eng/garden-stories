The Leaf That Took a Nap

a story for little ears and gentle hearts
🍂

High up in an old oak tree, a small green leaf clung to its branch. It had held on through spring rains and summer storms, through long, golden days and the slow arrival of autumn.

But one day, the leaf felt tired.

Not the kind of tired that comes from playing — but the kind that comes from knowing it was time to let go.

The leaf looked around. The other leaves were still bright and clinging. The wind was gentle, not rushing.

“Maybe I will stay a little longer,” the leaf thought.

But the branch beneath it felt different. It was not holding on as tightly as before. It seemed to be saying, softly, “It is time.”

The leaf whispered to the branch: “What if I fall? What if I am forgotten?”

And the branch, with a gentle creak, replied: “You will not be forgotten. The earth will hold you. And when you rest, you will become part of something new.”

The leaf waited a little longer. Then it let go — not with fear, but with trust.

It drifted down slowly, spinning softly, like a small, golden dancer. It landed on the ground, beside the roots of the tree. The earth was warm and soft, and the leaf lay still.

It was not the end. It was a nap. A long, quiet rest.

And as the seasons passed, the leaf became part of the soil — feeding the roots, helping the tree grow stronger. And in the spring, a new leaf appeared in its place, as if to say: “Thank you for making room.”

Sometimes rest is not giving up. It is making space for what comes next.
